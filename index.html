AfriFoundry's Methodology

How D.R.E.A.M. Works

The validation framework at the core of AfriFoundry's infrastructure.

What does the market actually need — not what the founder assumes? D is about getting out of your head and into the streets. Gikomba. Kongowea. Eastleigh. Real buyers, real pain, real gaps.
Remove every assumption that doesn't survive contact with real data. Most ideas die here — and that's the point. A refined idea is 10x more likely to launch successfully than a bloated one.
Does the solution respect the community it serves? No extraction. No exploitation. AfriFoundry refuses to validate ideas that profit by taking from the very people they claim to help.
Competition, pricing, demand, margin — against real African benchmarks. Not global averages. What does rent actually cost in Mombasa CBD? What margin is realistic in Gikomba? This is where AfriFoundry's 10,000+ datapoints become your competitive edge.
A validated idea with a clear path forward. Not just "this could work" — but here's how, here's where, here's what to do first. Confidence built on evidence, not hope.
AfriFoundry AI's Engine

The TEMIS Framework

The silent scoring engine behind every AfriFoundry AI conversation. Revealed in your validation report.

Kenya's infrastructure has specific constraints — power reliability, mobile internet coverage, last-mile logistics. An idea that works in Nairobi CBD may not work in Kisumu. TEMIS scores the technical viability against real local realities, not ideal conditions.
Margins, pricing power, end-of-month consumer behaviour. African purchasing patterns are different — peak spending is 1st–5th of the month. Does your unit economics survive the last week of the month when wallets are thin?
Supply chain reliability, talent availability, ongoing operational costs. Many African businesses survive launch but die at month 4 when founders discover the hidden cost of keeping things running. TEMIS stress-tests this before you invest.
Roads, power grid, mobile money penetration, cold chain, last-mile delivery. Infrastructure gaps that don't exist in a pitch deck become fatal on day one. TEMIS maps your idea against what actually exists on the ground.
Culture, trust dynamics, regulation, community structures, informal networks. Africa runs on relationships and trust. A solution that ignores how Kenyans actually make decisions — and who they trust — is a solution that won't get adopted.
